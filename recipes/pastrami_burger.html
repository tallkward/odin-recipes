Pastrami Burger

  1. 1 lb ground chuck
  2. 4 slices of pastrami
  3. 4 slices of your favorite cheese (such as cheddar or Swiss)
  4. 4 hamburger buns
  5. 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  6. 1 tbsp dijon mustard
  7. 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  8. 1 tsp garlic powder
  9. 1 tsp onion powder
  10. Salt and pepper, to taste
  11. Optional toppings: lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles
  12. 2 tbsp butter, divided

Instructions:

  1. Preheat a griddle or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Divide the ground chuck into 4 equal portions, then shape each portion into a ball.
  3. Melt 1 tablespoon of butter on the griddle or skillet. Place the balls of beef on the hot surface, then use a sturdy spatula to smash them down to a thin patty. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Cook the patties for about 2 minutes, until the edges start to crisp up and the bottom is browned and caramelized.
  5. Flip the patties, add a slice of cheese to each one, and cook for another 30 seconds to 1 minute, until the cheese is melted and the patties are cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  6. While the patties are cooking, heat the pastrami in a separate skillet until it is heated through and slightly crispy around the edges.
  7. Toast the buns on the griddle or skillet for a few seconds until they are lightly charred.
  8. In a small bowl, mix together the mayonnaise and dijon mustard to create a sauce.
  9. To assemble the burgers, spread the sauce on the bottom half of each bun. Add a slice of pastrami on top of the sauce, followed by the smash burger with melted cheese. Top with optional toppings such as lettuce, tomato, onion, or pickles if desired. Finish off with the top half of the bun.
  10. Serve the burgers hot and enjoy the delicious combination of pastrami, cheese, and crispy beef!
